## Session Insights and Key Breakthroughs

### Breakthrough 1: Fine-Tuning Is the Wrong Approach
The session surfaced that fine-tuning model weights is technically inferior to RAG over a structured Soul Document. Fine-tuned models become obsolete. Soul Documents are perpetually upgradeable and model-agnostic. This shifts NC's technical strategy significantly.

### Breakthrough 2: Time Is NC's Unique Differentiator
Every competitor models current state. NC's temporal snapshot stack models identity across time — the only system that can detect relationship drift before it becomes rupture. This is genuinely novel and deeply defensible.

### Breakthrough 3: Disagreement Is the Best Training Signal
Counterintuitive insight: the adversarial correction loop — where users disagree with their twin — produces higher-value training signal than agreement. Identity is defined by its edges, not its center. This inverts standard AI feedback design.

### Breakthrough 4: Neurodivergent Users Are the Proof of Concept
The gap between intent and expression is largest and most measurable in ND users. They're not a niche — they're the ideal first cohort: high need, high signal, loyal community, underserved by every existing tool.

### Breakthrough 5: Ethics Needs to Be Infrastructure, Not Policy
Personality data at this fidelity is a new legal category. NC should define the consent and governance standard before regulators do. "Personality Sovereignty" as a principle — not just a privacy policy.
