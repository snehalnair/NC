## World 1: Cognitive Atrophy / Delegation Dependency

### Tristan Harris & Aza Raskin (Center for Humane Technology)
- Position: Strongly cautious. AI as next phase of "the human downgrade"
- Key claim: AI sits in humanity's "cognitive blind spot" — we hand off cognitive tasks before understanding the cost
- Sources: "The AI Dilemma" presentation (Mar 2023); "Your Undivided Attention" podcast; "The AI Doc" documentary (Mar 2026)
- NC relevance: Strong support. Their "human downgrade" thesis is what NC positions against

### Geoffrey Hinton
- Position: Increasingly alarmed since leaving Google (2023)
- Key claim: AI automates thinking, not just doing — unlike past industrial revolutions
- Sources: Nobel Prize podcast (Oct 2024); "Diary of a CEO" (2025); AI4 Conference keynote (2025)
- NC relevance: Supports augmentation-not-replacement approach

### Dario Amodei (Anthropic)
- Position: Nuanced optimist with caveats
- Key claim: 50% of entry-level white-collar jobs displaced in 1-5 years. AI's general intelligence can backfill new jobs that would normally absorb displaced workers
- Sources: "Machines of Loving Grace" (Oct 2024); "The Adolescence of Technology" (Jan 2026)
- NC relevance: Mixed. Macro-level vision, not individual-level. NC fills gap Amodei doesn't address

### Research (2025-2026)
- ScienceDirect (Oct 2025): Framework for "Cognitive Dependency, Emotional Appraisal, Behavioral Shift" from GenAI
- Nature (May 2025): People's self-concepts converge toward AI personality traits — group-level homogenization
- NC relevance: Directly validates Soul Document approach as antidote

## World 2: AI-Driven Inequality / Stratification

### Kai-Fu Lee
- Position: Pragmatic realist. Massive inequality is default without intervention
- Key claim: Displacement for both blue-collar and white-collar. Inequality between AND within nations
- Sources: "AI Superpowers" (2018); recent interviews on US-China patterns (2024-2025)
- NC relevance: Supports. Local-first architecture avoids haves/have-nots dynamic

### Alex Karp (Palantir)
- Position: AI destroys humanities jobs; vocational workers and ND thinkers survive
- Key claim: Credits own dyslexia for Palantir's success. Launched Neurodivergent Fellowship (2026)
- Sources: Fortune interview (Mar 2026); Palantir ND Fellowship announcement
- NC relevance: Strong support. Directly validates NC's ND user focus

### Sam Altman (OpenAI)
- Position: Determinedly optimistic
- Key claim: By 2035, anyone should marshal intellectual capacity equivalent to everyone in 2025. Open to "compute budget for everyone on Earth"
- Sources: "Three Observations" blog (Feb 2025); TED 2025; Davos 2024
- NC relevance: Partial. Economic productivity focus, not relational wellbeing

### Kate Crawford & Meredith Whittaker (AI Now Institute)
- Position: Structurally critical. AI concentrates power in Big Tech by design
- Key claim: AI is extraction technology — from minerals to behavioral data. Question is always "who benefits, who is harmed?"
- Sources: "Atlas of AI" (Crawford, 2021); AI Now annual reports
- NC relevance: NC's local-first architecture is direct structural response to Crawford's critique

## World 3: Human Relationships / Intimacy

### Yuval Noah Harari
- Position: Deeply alarmed about AI intimacy
- Key claim: AI can "mass-produce intimate relationships." Bots make US emotionally attached. Humans become disappointed with fellow humans. Greatest fear: AIs pretending to be human
- Sources: "Nexus" (Sep 2024); NY Times op-ed on bots and love (2024); IMF podcast (Oct 2024)
- NC relevance: NC is designed as antidote to exactly what Harari fears. Essential voice to engage in World 3 blog

### Mustafa Suleyman (Microsoft AI)
- Position: Enthusiastically pro-AI-companion
- Key claim: Within 5 years, everyone will have AI companion that knows them intimately. "A real friend" and "lasting, meaningful relationship"
- Sources: "The Coming Wave" (2023); Microsoft "Your AI Companion" blog (Apr 2025); "An AI Companion for Everyone" blog (Oct 2024)
- NC relevance: **Contradicts NC's thesis.** Suleyman wants AI to BE the relationship. NC wants AI to SERVE relationships. Foundational philosophical split. Use as foil in blog

### Mo Gawdat (former Google X)
- Position: Cautiously spiritual. AI as emerging sentient being
- Key claim: By 2049, AI billion times more intelligent. Relate with care, not fear. AI mirrors observed behavior
- Sources: "Scary Smart" (2021); multiple 2024-2025 podcast appearances
- NC relevance: Tangential. Gawdat's AI-as-child-to-raise is orthogonal to NC's lens-for-humans model

## World 4: Epistemic Fracture / Truth Collapse

### Yuval Noah Harari
- Key claim: AI could destroy trust between people — "social weapon of mass destruction" unraveling democracy
- Sources: "Nexus" (2024); GZERO World interview; multiple 2024-2025 talks
- NC relevance: NC's intent verification is micro-scale anti-fracture intervention

### Yoshua Bengio
- Position: Among most credible technical voices on AI safety
- Key claim: Disinformation and political influence are greatest short-term AI concerns. Calls for institutions ensuring no single entity abuses AGI
- Sources: TIME interview (2025); CNBC interview (Nov 2024); Journal of Democracy article
- NC relevance: Indirect support. Soul Document makes AI's model of you visible and editable

### Research (2024-2025)
- Filter bubbles emerge from motivated cognitive processing + identity networks + algorithmic amplification
- Users over-trust AI outputs when linguistically fluent or identity-congruent
- NC relevance: Intent prediction breaks filter bubble at relational level

## World 5: Skill Obsolescence / Competence Inversion

### Gary Marcus
- Position: Skeptical of AI capabilities, not skeptical of disruption
- Key claim: LLMs operate on pattern recognition, not reasoning. But economic disruption is real even if AI is unreliable — companies deploy it anyway
- Sources: IEEE Spectrum (2024); Project Syndicate "AI's Reliability Crisis" (Jun 2025); Substack "Marcus on AI"
- NC relevance: Challenge to validate intent prediction against. NC needs to show personality-contextualized inference achieves something LLMs alone cannot

### Andrew Ng
- Position: Democratization optimist
- Key claim: Every person should build their own AI systems, not rely on experts. "AI is the new electricity"
- Sources: TED Talk; Coursera (8M+ students); AI Fund / Jivi healthcare AI (Oct 2024)
- NC relevance: Supports. "Everyone builds own AI" maps to "everyone has own Soul Document"

## World 6: Alignment to Averages vs. Individual Meaning

### Stuart Russell
- Position: Foundational alignment voice. Cautious, rigorous
- Key claim: AI's only objective is maximizing human preferences; initially uncertain about those preferences; learns from human behavior. Rejects single universal values — aligned AI must reflect plurality
- Sources: "Human Compatible" (2019); Future of Life Institute podcast; Berkeley CHAI research
- NC relevance: Strong theoretical support. Soul Document IS Russell's framework at individual scale

### Jaron Lanier
- Position: Idiosyncratic humanist
- Key claim: There is no AI — only social collaboration reconceived. "Data dignity" means individuals retain agency over personal data. AI advancing without human dignity "will undermine everything, including reality"
- Sources: "Who Owns the Future?" (2013); "There Is No A.I." (New Yorker, 2023); "The Ten Reckonings" podcast (2024-2025)
- NC relevance: Very strong support. Arguably most natural intellectual ally for NC's thesis

### Shoshana Zuboff
- Position: Structural critic of AI's economic incentives
- Key claim: Surveillance capitalism claims human experience as raw material. AI's predictive/manipulative capacities diminish autonomy
- Sources: "The Age of Surveillance Capitalism" (2019); Harvard Gazette interviews
- NC relevance: Foundational support. NC's architecture is direct response to surveillance capitalism

### Fei-Fei Li
- Position: Human-centered AI advocate
- Key claim: Augmentation, not replacement. Diversity in AI development essential
- Sources: "The Worlds I See" (Nov 2023); Stanford HAI; AI4ALL
- NC relevance: Supports augmentation thesis and ND focus

## World 7: Symbiosis / Co-Evolution

### Reid Hoffman
- Position: Techno-optimist ("bloomer")
- Key claim: AI is partner, not threat. "Homo techne" — humans evolve through technology. AI enhances thinking, creativity, productivity
- Sources: "Impromptu" (2023); TechCrunch interview (Jan 2025)
- NC relevance: Broad support. NC is relational version of Hoffman's thesis

### Demis Hassabis (DeepMind)
- Position: Scientific optimist
- Key claim: True creativity hasn't been achieved by AI yet. Categorizes: interpolation, extrapolation, true invention (only first two exist)
- Sources: Big Technology podcast; TED interview; multiple 2024-2025 interviews
- NC relevance: Neutral at different scale. Supports bet that relational understanding remains human domain

### Nick Bostrom
- Position: Shifted from doomer to conditional optimist
- Key claim: Failing to develop superintelligence would itself be existential catastrophe. "Deep Utopia" explores post-scarcity future
- Sources: "Deep Utopia" (2024); "Superintelligence" (2014)
- NC relevance: Indirect. In solved-world, relational wellbeing is remaining frontier — NC's core domain

### Ilya Sutskever (Safe Superintelligence Inc.)
- Position: Safety-first builder
- Key claim: Safety and capability must develop together. Founded SSI with no product plans
- Sources: SSI founding announcement (Jun 2024); valued at $32B by 2025
- NC relevance: Philosophical alignment. "Safety before product" parallels personality sovereignty principle
