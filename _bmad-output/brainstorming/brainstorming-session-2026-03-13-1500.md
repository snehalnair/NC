## Phase 3: First Principles Thinking

### Bedrock Truths (stripped to fundamentals)

**Truth 1 — What communication IS:**
Communication = transfer of internal state between two minds via imperfect symbols. Misunderstanding is the DEFAULT, not the exception.

**Truth 2 — What a misunderstanding IS:**
Misunderstanding = delta between sender intent and receiver interpretation.
Two sources: (A) encoding noise, (B) threat-hijacked decoding.

**Truth 3 — The root cause (neuroscience):**
The amygdala processes incoming signals **40–80ms faster** than the PFC. Threat interpretation precedes semantic interpretation by design. For ambiguous signals, the brain defaults to threat — evolutionarily cheaper to be wrong about safety than wrong about danger. Most "communication problems" are not encoding failures. They are **decoding failures caused by threat-response hijacking semantic processing**.

**Truth 4 — What a solution must do:**
Intervene in the amygdala-PFC timing gap. Three intervention moments:
- Moment 1 (before sending): reduce encoding noise — achievable in v1
- Moment 2 (before reading): pre-process signal for intent before threat activates — the fundamental breakthrough, hardest, most powerful
- Moment 3 (after receiving): post-hoc threat reappraisal — most feasible in v1

**Truth 5 — THE ONE HYPOTHESIS (bedrock):**
> *"Linguistic, contextual, and relational features of a message contain sufficient information to model the probability distribution of communicative intent — independently of surface tone — with accuracy meaningfully above human baseline in ambiguous communication scenarios."*

### The Proposal Opening Paragraph (First Principles version)
> *"Human miscommunication is not a social problem — it is a neuroscientific one. The amygdala processes incoming signals 40–80ms before the prefrontal cortex begins semantic interpretation, meaning threat-response hijacks meaning-making by default. No computational model of this process exists. We propose to build one: a NeuroAI foundation model grounded in the neural circuitry of pragmatic inference — trained to model the probability distribution of communicative intent from linguistic, contextual, and relational features — that operates at the precise intersection of neuroscience and computational linguistics where this problem lives. The cognitive companion is the scientific instrument. The rewired human is the goal."*
