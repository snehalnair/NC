## Research Overview

This report synthesises four converging evidence streams:
1. Inter-brain coupling as the neural substrate of social relationship formation

3. Amygdala–prefrontal circuit rebalancing via targeted intervention
4. Closed-loop feedback as a training signal for mentalising circuitry (TPJ)

## 1. Inter-Brain Coupling: The Neural Substrate of Trust Formation

**Key finding:** Inter-brain synchrony is *plastic* — it persists beyond the interaction that creates it, and is the biological mechanism by which social closeness develops.

A 2024 study (PMC10825012) directly investigated whether inter-brain coupling is plastic and carries forward after interaction. Key results:
- Heightened inter-brain coupling in the **inferior frontal gyrus (IFG)** and **dorsomedial prefrontal cortex** persisted post-interaction compared to pre-task baseline
- Post-task coupling in the **right IFG specifically predicted motivation to connect socially** — a trust-adjacent behavioural marker
- The effect is measurable with EEG (theta-band phase synchrony), not only fMRI

A complementary review (Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 2022; PubMed 36092652) formalised this as **inter-brain plasticity**: recurring exposure to high inter-brain synchrony produces lasting change in a person's *overall capacity* for synchrony. This is the mechanism by which therapy "sticks" — and the mechanism H₃ proposes to activate via 60-day intent feedback.

Singer (2025, NYAS) extends this: the social and relational brain is plastic across the lifespan; structural thickness increases in TPJ and posterior parietal cortex follow socio-cognitive training.

**Implication for H₃:** The 60-day intent feedback loop is a repeated exposure to high-synchrony-state conditions (the user sees their own intent matched to its likely reception, reducing the mismatch that creates dysynchrony). If the inter-brain plasticity model holds, this should produce lasting upward shift in the user's baseline synchrony capacity — measurable as IBS increase at Day 60.

## 2. Oxytocin: The Neurochemical Lever for Trust-Directed Plasticity

**Key finding:** Oxytocin is both the *signal* of successful trust and the *substrate* for the neuroplastic changes that encode it — it modulates the same circuits the Companion targets.

Evidence from oxytocin and neural plasticity research (PMC8604207; multiple 2024 reviews):

| Mechanism | Effect | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Oxytocin + dopamine co-release in nucleus accumbens | Reinforces social bonding behaviours | Creates positive valence around trust-confirming interactions |
| Oxytocin signalling in **medial amygdala** | Critical for social memory formation | Reduces threat-primed responses to known individuals |
| Oxytocin modulation of HPA axis | Reduces cortisol; downregulates stress response | Directly counters the amygdala threat-first parsing mechanism |
| Low-trust relationships → lower oxytocin, higher cortisol | Chronic trust deficit has a measurable neurochemical signature | The intent gap is not just behavioural — it has a hormonal cost |
| Oxytocin facilitates neuroplasticity itself | Enables structural/functional brain changes in sustained social states | Closing the intent gap may trigger its own oxytocin-mediated entrenchment |

**Key implication:** The intent gap is not merely a communication error. It is a cortisol-generating, oxytocin-suppressing event. Every unresolved intent misread has a small but real neurochemical cost. Conversely, every time the Companion correctly scaffolds the user's mentalising — and the user experiences *recognised intent* — the neurochemical direction inverts. The biological change is happening in both directions already. The Companion tips the balance.

## 3. Amygdala–Prefrontal Rebalancing: The Core Structural Target

**Key finding:** The amygdala–prefrontal axis is *reversible* through intervention. CBT produces measurable grey matter volume reduction in the amygdala and increased lateral PFC activation — the exact rebalancing H₃ predicts.

Evidence from intervention studies:

- **Bilateral prefrontal cortex stimulation** significantly reduced right amygdala threat response (biorxiv 215699)
- **CBT-induced amygdala grey matter reduction** correlated positively with reduced anticipatory anxiety; time × treatment interactions showed both volume *and* BOLD responsivity decreased post-treatment (diva-portal 877737)
- Structural changes confirmed: lateral PFC (executive control) upregulation + amygdala downregulation = the measurable neural signature of successful threat-response rebalancing

**The circuit model for H₃:**

```
Baseline state (high intent gap):
  Amygdala: high activation on emotionally ambiguous messages
  TPJ/mPFC: low activation (mentalising suppressed by threat cascade)
  Net: threat-first parsing; intent gap wide

Post-intervention state (60-day closed loop):
  Amygdala: reduced threat activation on familiar ambiguous patterns
  TPJ/mPFC: increased activation (mentalising scaffolded repeatedly)
  Net: mentalising-first parsing; intent gap narrowed

Measurable as: IBS increase + ERP latency shift (N2/P3)
```

The pathway is not speculative — it is the standard threat-extinction / emotion-regulation neuroplasticity model, applied to a novel stimulus category (digital text messages) with a novel feedback mechanism (intent distribution display).

## 4. Closed-Loop Feedback and TPJ Training: The Mechanism of the Companion

**Key finding:** Closed-loop feedback (neurofeedback architecture) is an established mechanism for training self-regulation of specific brain regions and behaviours. The TPJ is trainable. Social brain plasticity follows socio-cognitive training with measurable structural correlates.

From Nature Reviews Neuroscience (neurofeedback review, PubMed 28003656):
- In neurofeedback, online feedback of neural activity facilitates self-regulation of a target brain region and produces lasting behavioural change
- The mechanism is Hebbian: repeated co-activation of the target circuit and the feedback signal strengthens the circuit's baseline activation threshold

From training-induced social brain plasticity research:
- **TPJ thickness increases** correlated with individual differences in Theory of Mind (ToM) performance improvements after training (Cambridge Core — ASD plasticity study)
- Increases overlapped precisely with functional activations during socio-cognitive tasks — structural and functional change are co-localised
- Excitatory TMS/tDCS to right TPJ *enhances* perspective-taking; disruptive stimulation hinders it — causal evidence for TPJ's role

From inter-brain plasticity in empathic learning (Frontiers Psychology, 2022):
- As empathic interactions continue, the empathiser learns to adapt based on feedback; inter-brain networks reconfigure accordingly
- The Companion is a proxy empathic signal: the model's intent distribution IS the feedback signal that normally requires a responsive human partner

**The key theoretical claim this grounds:** The Companion does not require the *other person* to be present or responsive to produce the training signal. The model-provided intent distribution serves as the corrective feedback normally provided by a skilled empathic interlocutor. This is the scientific argument for why a digital tool can produce the inter-brain plasticity effect that normally requires a human relationship.

## Synthesis: What Are the Key Levers to Alter Neuroplasticity in a Trust-Based Direction?

Four converging levers — all of which the Cognitive Companion activates:

| Lever | Mechanism | How the Companion activates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Repeated high-synchrony exposure** | Inter-brain plasticity: sustained exposure raises baseline synchrony capacity | 60 days of intent-matched feedback = repeated synchrony-state induction |
| **Oxytocin release via recognised intent** | Every correctly scaffolded intent = small oxytocin/dopamine release in trust circuit | Model's accurate intent display triggers the neurochemical reward of "being understood" |
| **Amygdala downregulation via PFC reappraisal** | Cognitive reappraisal window (Gross 1998) activates lateral PFC, suppresses amygdala cascade | The pause between receipt and response IS the reappraisal window; the intent display fills it |
| **TPJ structural strengthening via closed-loop feedback** | Neurofeedback architecture: repeated co-activation of feedback signal + TPJ target | Intent distribution display = the feedback signal; mentalising = the target behaviour being trained |

**The minimum viable biological argument for H₃:**

> *If the closed-loop intent feedback display (a) occupies the cognitive reappraisal window, (b) repeatedly activates the TPJ via forced perspective-taking, and (c) reduces amygdala-dominant parsing through consistent corrective signal delivery — then 60 days of this produces measurable structural and functional rebalancing of the amygdala–TPJ axis. This is the same mechanism as CBT-induced amygdala change and TPJ-training-induced thickness increase. The delivery vehicle is novel. The neuroscience is not.*
